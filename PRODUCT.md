# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Local regulars and neighborhood newcomers: people who walk by, hear about it from a friend, or search nearby cafes. Their context is casual discovery, often on mobile, often deciding in the moment whether to come in. They want to know the vibe, hours, and roughly what's on the menu before they show up.

## Product Purpose

A public-facing marketing site for a neighborhood cafe. It exists to make people want to walk through the door. Success is foot traffic; the site is the first impression.

## Brand Personality

Warm, unpretentious, and genuinely welcoming. The kind of place where everyone feels at home from the first visit. Not exclusive, not corporate, not trying too hard. Like a friend's kitchen that happens to make great coffee.

Three words: **warm, real, yours**.

## Anti-references

- Generic food delivery apps (Uber Eats, DoorDash UI): no transactional-first layouts, no star ratings in grids, no "add to cart" as the primary action.

## Design Principles

1. **Show up, not sell.** Every element should invite, not convert. Resist calls-to-action that feel pushy or transactional.
2. **Everyone's welcome.** The design should feel as easy for a first-timer as for a daily regular. No insider language, no gatekeeping.
3. **The place before the product.** Atmosphere and feeling come first. The vibe pulls people in before the menu does.
4. **Local over polished.** A little texture, a little human imperfection. Warmth over corporate precision.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Best-effort. No formal WCAG level required, but maintain readable contrast and legible type throughout.
